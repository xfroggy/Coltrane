John Coltrane

A Love Supreme

Timeline of John Coltrane's life:

  • September 23, 1926 - Born in Hamlet, North Carolina
  • July 13, 1946 - First recordings, with Navy musicians
  • June 5, 1945 - Saw Charlie Parker perform for the first time.
  • October 1955 - April 1957 - with Miles Davis in the "First Great Quintet"
  • July - December 1957 - Thelonious Monk's quartet at Five Spot Cafe
  • January 1958 - Rejoins Miles Davis, now a sextet
  • 1959 - Recorded Giant Steps, containing altered chord progression cycles that became known as the Coltrane changes.
  • 1961-1962 - Early years with Impluse! Records. Coltrane radically altered his style. Critics and audiences were perplexed.
  • 1962-1965 - "The Classic Quartet", expanded his improvisations, but on stage continually reworking his standards
  • 1965 - Avant-garde jazz and the second quartet
  • 1965-1967 - regularly adding other free jazz musicians to his group, but angered some members who left
  • July 17, 1967 - died of liver cancer in Long Island
